**Problem.** Why does the cross-cultural difference in reference intuitions (East Asians more descriptivist than Westerners) appear robustly in the Gödel case but not in other probes like Jonah or Twin Earth?

## Conclusion

The Gödel effect is driven by an interaction between linguistic structure and probe architecture. English's definite article "the" presupposes uniqueness and forces speakers to resolve between competing description types (brute-fact discovery vs. social-fact naming); Mandarin and Japanese classifiers encode identifiability without uniqueness presupposition, permitting sustained ambiguity. The Gödel scenario's counterfactual structure naturally invokes Mandarin's demonstrative-based pragmatic encoding strategy for counterfactuals (Yong 2016), which licenses ambiguity tolerance unavailable in English. The effect is Gödel-specific because only Gödel structurally requires simultaneous reference to two competing descriptions of a single individual under uncertainty; Jonah and Twin Earth lack this dual-description architecture. This mechanism is necessary but not sufficient—response-style bias (Henrich et al. 2010, Barrett et al. 2013) and culturally mediated perspective-taking norms amplify the effect, but do not explain why it attaches to Gödel alone.

## Confidence
**0.58** — The linguistic mechanism is theoretically coherent and supported by consistent findings in three domains (classifier semantics, counterfactual encoding, case-specificity), but the critical test—administering the Gödel probe in native Mandarin/Japanese with explicit demonstratives while preserving the dual-description structure—has not been conducted. Until that test is run, the mechanism remains a well-motivated hypothesis rather than confirmed fact. Additionally, the claim requires integration with perspective-ambiguity effects (Sytsma & Livengood 2012; Machery 2015) and must account for Ding & Liu (2022)'s finding that the effect weakens in native Mandarin translation, a counterintuitive result the linguistic account does not yet explain.

## Key reasons and evidence

**Linguistic foundation: Mandarin classifiers encode familiarity, not uniqueness.**
- Li et al. (2009) and subsequent linguistic consensus: Mandarin definite reference is characterized by pragmatic "familiarity/identifiability," not the uniqueness presupposition that English's "the" carries.
- English forces binary resolution of competing descriptions via uniqueness; Mandarin permits "that individual identifiable under either description" as a coherent response.
- This difference is direct and measurable in semantic presupposition theory; it is not a discourse or pragmatic inference.

**Case-specificity confirmed at scale.**
- Recent large-scale replication (2025, cited in Index 10): Over 2,000 Mandarin speakers tested across five online studies. The Gödel case "peculiarly stands out" in ability to elicit robust cultural differences (~65% East Asian descriptivist vs. ~56.5% Western). The effect is weak, absent, or reversed for Jonah and Twin Earth.
- Machery et al. (2004) original: 65% E. Asian descriptivist on Gödel vs. 50% Western; near-zero effect on other probes.
- Beebe & Undercoffer (2016) controlled for anchoring bias and confirmed effect persists: Gödel remains outlier.

**Gödel scenario encodes dual competing descriptions.**
- Ding & Liu (2022): The core ambiguity is structural—subjects must choose between "brute-fact description" (who actually discovered the incompleteness theorems) and "social-fact description" (whose name is attached to the discovery in the scientific community). This is not merely epistemic uncertainty; it is a classification ambiguity at the level of description type.
- Sytsma & Livengood (2012): Identified a "perspective ambiguity" in the original probe: subjects diverge based on whether they adopt the speaker's referential intention vs. the semantic reference of the name. Machery (2015) conducted clarification experiments to remove this ambiguity; the effect persists even after perspective is disambiguated (though magnitude may slightly reduce).
- Jonah and Twin Earth do not encode this dual-description structure. Jonah is resolved pragmatically via loose association; Twin Earth is resolved via predicate-extension and context-shift.

**Mandarin counterfactuals rely on demonstratives and pragmatic flexibility.**
- Yong (2016) corpus study: Mandarin counterfactuals are positively correlated with demonstratives, negation, emphatic modals, and optative mood. Mandarin lacks obligatory tense, aspect, or subjunctive morphemes to mark counterfactuality; instead, it uses pragmatic and lexical markers that remain semantically underdetermined.
- The Gödel scenario is fundamentally counterfactual ("suppose the person who got Schmidt's manuscript and published under a false name..."). In Mandarin, this counterfactual framing naturally invokes the demonstrative-pragmatic encoding strategy, which does not enforce uniqueness resolution.
- This is a structural match: the probe's counterfactual force and Mandarin's default counterfactual encoding both permit and sustain ambiguity.

**Response-style bias is documented but does not explain specificity.**
- Henrich et al. (2010) and Barrett et al. (2013): East Asian respondents show higher acquiescence bias, modesty bias, and caution under uncertainty.
- In principle, this could inflate descriptivist responses across all probes. However, the effect is Gödel-specific, suggesting response-style bias amplifies but does not originate the difference. Response-style bias is most pronounced when cognitive load and interpretive ambiguity are high—exactly the Gödel case. Jonah and Twin Earth, while pragmatically complex, do not trigger the same dual-description uncertainty that invokes caution.

**Perspective-ambiguity partially explains but does not fully determine the pattern.**
- Sytsma & Livengood (2012) and Machery (2015): When perspective ambiguity is removed, the effect shrinks but persists. This indicates that perspective-taking norms interact with the probe structure but are not the whole story.
- Ding & Liu (2022): The perspective question maps onto brute-fact vs. social-fact description-type ambiguity. Subjects diverge not merely on "whose perspective" but on "what type of fact resolves the reference." This is more fine-grained than pure perspective ambiguity.

**Unexpected finding: Effect weakens in native Mandarin translation (Ding & Liu 2022).**
- When the probe is administered in native Mandarin (not English translated), the cross-cultural difference reduces. This is counterintuitive to the linguistic mechanism as formulated and requires explanation. Possible post-hoc account: in written Chinese, pragmatic triggers for counterfactuality (demonstratives, emphatic modals) may be less salient than in English paraphrasing; this could reduce the pragmatic ambiguity license. However, this remains speculative and untested.

## What would falsify this

**Test 1: Native-language demonstrative test (critical).**
- Administer the Gödel probe in native Mandarin/Japanese using forced-choice demonstratives ("那個人" / "that specific person who really discovered X" vs. "that person who got Schmidt's work") while preserving the brute-fact/social-fact ambiguity structure.
- **If** the cross-cultural effect persists at ≥60% of original magnitude, the uniqueness-presupposition mechanism is not operative, and the effect is primarily cognitive/cultural.
- **If** the effect shrinks to <30% of original magnitude, linguistic structure is causally operative.

**Test 2: Probe-structure variant manipulation.**
- Construct Variant A: Remove the dual-description structure from a Gödel-like counterfactual scenario (e.g., "Suppose Schmidt got the credit instead of Gödel. Who is the referent of 'Gödel'?" — single description, no contrast). Prediction: effect disappears.
- Construct Variant B: Add a dual-description structure to a Jonah-like scenario (e.g., "Jonah was called 'the whale swallower' by his community, but actually a storm swallowed the boat. Who does 'Jonah' refer to?" — dual descriptions, loose pragmatics). Prediction: effect emerges.
- **If** these manipulations produce predicted results, description-structure specificity is confirmed. **If** they fail to produce effects, the mechanism is not description-structure specificity.

**Test 3: Cross-linguistic systematic matrix.**
- Test languages varying in article systems and uniqueness presupposition:
  - **English, French** (obligatory articles + uniqueness): Predict high cross-cultural difference.
  - **Mandarin, Japanese** (classifiers/demonstratives, no uniqueness): Predict low cross-cultural difference.
  - **Hindi, Turkish** (no articles, but different aspectual/evidential systems): Predict intermediate.
- **If** effect correlates with uniqueness-presupposition strength across languages, linguistic mechanism is supported. **If** effect correlates with response-style cultural factors independent of language structure, mechanism is falsified.

**Test 4: Processing-level evidence (eye-tracking / reaction time).**
- Measure fixation durations, regressive saccades, and reaction times when English and Mandarin speakers process "the person who really discovered" (or native equivalent).
- **Prediction:** English speakers show longer fixations and more regressions (processing pressure to resolve uniqueness); Mandarin speakers show sustained engagement without forced resolution.
- **If** predictions hold, the mechanism operates at the processing level. **If** no processing difference emerges, mechanism requires revision.

**Test 5: Developmental test in native language (no L2 exposure).**
- Test monolingual Mandarin-speaking children (ages 5–7, no English exposure) and English-speaking peers on the native-language Gödel probe.
- **If** monolingual Mandarin children show equivalent descrip tivist bias to English-exposed peers, the effect is not mediated by linguistic structure but by cultural/cognitive factors, and the mechanism is falsified.
- **If** monolingual Mandarin children show markedly lower descriptivist responses than English-exposed peers, linguistic structure is operative from early development.

**Test 6: Perspective-ambiguity removal.**
- Reword the probe to eliminate all perspective ambiguity: "According to the semantic conventions of [language], to whom does the name 'Gödel' refer in this scenario?"
- **If** the cross-cultural effect drops below 15 percentage points or disappears, perspective ambiguity, not linguistic structure, is primary.
- **If** effect persists at >40 percentage points, linguistic mechanism survives perspective disambiguation.

## Directions for further research

1. **Priority 1: Run the native-language demonstrative test.** This is the lynchpin. Partner with linguistically sophisticated researchers in Taiwan/Mainland China and Japan to administer the Gödel probe in native Mandarin/Japanese with explicit classifier-marked or demonstrative-marked definite descriptions. Replicate Machery et al.'s original design in parallel (English-translated version and native-language version with equivalent semantic content). This single study will determine whether the linguistic mechanism is operative.

2. **Priority 2: Construct and test Variants A and B.** Design a tightly controlled experiment isolating the "dual competing descriptions" structural feature. Test whether removing this feature (Variant A) eliminates the effect, and whether adding it to Jonah-like probes (Variant B) creates an effect. This will isolate whether case-specificity is driven by probe architecture or by other factors.

3. **Priority 3: Process-level investigation.** Conduct eye-tracking studies with English and Mandarin speakers on the Gödel probe (native language for each) to measure processing signatures of "forced uniqueness resolution" vs. "sustained ambiguity tolerance." This will test whether the mechanism operates at the comprehension/processing level or is post-hoc rationalization.

4. **Priority 4: Cross-linguistic matrix with careful linguistic classification.** Test the hypothesized relationship between article-system strength (obligatory uniqueness presupposition) and cross-cultural effect magnitude across a diverse set of languages (Hindi, Turkish, Korean, Thai, Russian). Measure effect magnitude controlling for response-style cultural dimensions using validated instruments (Barrett et al., Henrich et al.). This will determine whether the linguistic variable predicts effect size independently of cultural response-style bias.

5. **Secondary: Mechanistic integration with perspective-ambiguity and brute-fact/social-fact description types.** Ding & Liu (2022) mapped perspective ambiguity onto description-type ambiguity; future work should formalize this mapping and test whether resolving one dimension (e.g., forcing semantic reference via explicit instruction) automatically resolves the other (description type). This would clarify the relationship between linguistic and conceptual structure.

6. **Secondary: Account for the weakening of the effect in native Mandarin (Ding & Liu 2022).** Conduct a direct comparison of English-translated vs. native-Mandarin versions of the probe, with manipulation of pragmatic markers (demonstratives, emphatic modals) to identify whether counterfactual salience or pragmatic licensing is the key variable. If pragmatic triggering is critical, this reframes the mechanism from "linguistic presupposition" to "pragmatic licensing of ambiguity."

7. **Exploratory: Test whether response-style bias is truly secondary or is primary for a subset of subjects.** Collect response-style questionnaire data (acquiescence, modesty, uncertainty avoidance) from the same subjects who answer the Gödel probe. Conduct mediation analysis: does controlling for response-style measures reduce the cross-cultural effect? If effect remains large after controlling for measured response-style, linguistic/structural mechanisms are confirmed to be primary; if effect shrinks substantially, response-style bias may be underestimated in current literature.

## Open disagreements in the cell

**Disagreement 1: Whether linguistic structure is causal or merely permissive.**

The Disruptor emphasizes that linguistic structure (classifiers without uniqueness presupposition) is *causally operative*—Mandarin speakers process the Gödel probe differently because their language permits ambiguity tolerance that English does not. The mechanism is real and mechanistic.

The Architect is more cautious: linguistic structure may be *necessary but not sufficient*. The perspective ambiguity and cultural norms about how to resolve perspective under uncertainty might be primary; language merely permits (does not force) divergent interpretations. The weakening of the effect in native Mandarin (Ding & Liu 2022) suggests that when linguistic factors are brought into sharper focus, the effect may shrink, implying cultural resolution norms, not linguistic causation, drive the difference.

**Resolution condition:** The native-language demonstrative test (Priority 1) will definitively settle this. If the effect *persists* in native Mandarin with explicit demonstratives, linguistic structure is causal. If it *shrinks*, language is permissive but not primary.

**Disagreement 2: Whether the mechanism should be framed as "uniqueness presupposition avoidance" or "brute-fact/social-fact description-type ambiguity tolerance."**

The Disruptor's original formulation focused on uniqueness presupposition as the core mechanism. However, Ding & Liu (2022) identified that the underlying ambiguity is more precisely about competing *description types* (brute fact vs. social fact), not purely about uniqueness presupposition. The Architect advocates for the more precise framing.

The Disruptor concedes this is more linguistically accurate but argues that *why* description-type ambiguity is tolerated in Mandarin but not English is still explained by the uniqueness-presupposition difference: English's "the" forces uniqueness, which entails resolving which description type uniquely identifies; Mandarin permits "that person identifiable under either description" without uniqueness resolution, thus the description type can remain ambiguous.

**Consensus:** Both positions are compatible. The mechanism is "uniqueness-presupposition avoidance *under dual descriptions*," with the Architect's precise framing preferred for publication.
